# Yuragi (揺らぎ) — Framework Integration Note

## Status
Resolved: **yuragi is a phenomenological descriptor and enabling condition, not a new phase or stratum.**

## Placement in the Architecture of Taste

The six-phase framework remains intact:

1. Address
2. Discrimination
3. Tension
4. Commitment
5. Consummation
6. Regeneration

Yuragi names the **texture of wavering** inside and across these phases. It is the felt quality of:

- the slight irregularity that makes an address catch attention (Address)
- the fine variation that makes comparison possible (Discrimination)
- the wavering between "this works" and "this could be more right" (Tension)
- the continued uncertainty against which a direction is chosen (Commitment)
- the honoring of the écart rather than its erasure (Consummation)
- the warm afterimage of fluctuation that makes return meaningful (Regeneration)

It is also the **enabling condition of phase transitions**: controlled fluctuation lets a nonlinear discriminator detect the weak signal of the almost-right. Without yuragi, the gap is invisible; with too much, judgment dissolves; with the right amount, the almost-right becomes audible.

## Analogies used in the book

- **Stochastic resonance:** background noise lets a nonlinear system detect weak signals.
- **Dither:** intentional noise preserves detail that quantization would flatten.
- **Kaneko's *Yuragi* textile:** two materials shrink at different rates; the undulation is the design.
- **Hirai's stressed clay:** imperfection is a record of transformation, not an accident.
- **Morandi's repeated bottles:** near-sameness without final convergence.

## AI-native forms

- Session boundaries: sharp re-encounter with the past, not continuous memory.
- Output sampling: irreducible token-level variation.
- Temperature: the amplitude dial for generative noise.
- Multi-agent encounter: external perturbation that moves one's horizon.
